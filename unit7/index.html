How did you define your audience and tone?

My audience will consist of my previous employers, clients, and colleagues as well as prospective clients, industry enthusiasts, and professionals (e.g., graphic designers, creative directors, and developers).

I wanted the tone to be professional, but casual.

What existing websites out there were you most inspired by?

For this project, I was most inspired by http://www.alistairlane.com/. A lot of websites look similar these days with the templates available. I found his site to be interesting. I like the way he presented his content and that the site doesn’t have the standard navigation menu on the top of the page. The site is clean, minimalist, and has a monochromatic color palette.

What parts of your design are you most proud of?

I’m most proud of the scrolling speed and my “back to top” button. It fades in as you scroll down the page then fades out as you scroll up the page.

What struggles did you encounter in your design process?

I like clean and minimalist design, which sometimes can feel too cold and too formal. So my challenge is to find the right combo to maintain a professional look while being more casual. Exploring with fonts, colors, and layouts. To be continued…

What parts of the development process did you overcome?

  • Some of the effects for the site were written with jQuery, a language that I haven’t learned or used before.

  • I also added a favicon image that had to be saved under the .ico format. I didn’t realize at first that I had to download a plugin to Photoshop to get that option.

  • The following tags were not working: "em" and "strong." I was sure of their meaning ("em" being italic, and "strong" being bold, by default) but they were not changing the style of the font until I defined them in css. I guess the values were cancelled with the “reset.css” file.

What struggles did you encounter when developing the page?

The navigation and footer don't look as they should on Safari.

What did you learn from working through this mid-term project?

Detach yourself from what you like and your initial concept. You might be drawn to a font, a color, or a layout, but does it work for the specific project that you’re working on? Detach yourself from it, move on, and be flexible.

Each element must have a reason behind it.
